Exhibition Caption:


Layered Figures Series: Four Chairs

・To treat the negative ground surrounding an actively expressed figure, for example “a,” as something substantial in itself, which we may call “non-a.”
・Not to assume that the ground “non-a,” which emerges when figure “a” is drawn, and the ground “non-b,” which emerges when figure “b” is drawn, are the same space.
・To layer different ground regions themselves, such as “non-a,” “non-b,” and “non-c,” rather than the figures.

From Kazuo Sakata’s Composition

Four chairs from the “Layered Figures Series” are placed in the venue. Each chair is a three-dimensional realization, in one of four patterns, of a single figure created by layering images of multiple chairs. In other words, they share the same elevation.
Multiple images overlap, interfering with and emphasizing one another while also yielding to one another, so that they coexist within a single figure. By combining two types of members, each image appears differently according to depth and to the way one takes precedence over another.

Thus, the form is never fixed as one. Each time one sits, and each time one perceives it, a different image emerges, compelling us to read it in another way.
