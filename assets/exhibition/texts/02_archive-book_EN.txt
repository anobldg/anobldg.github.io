Ano bldg Archive Book / Preface: From the City / Opening:


In December 2024, the research we had begun in Nihonbashi Yokoyama-cho would later develop into Ano bldg.

From the day we decided to make an architectural proposal in this neighborhood, Nihonbashi Yokoyama-cho was no longer merely a bleak part of the city. The façades along the street, which we had hardly noticed before, began to detach themselves one by one, emerging as objects of observation.
As we walked through the city, a glittering window frame caught our eye. An old ocher-colored iron plate had been cut into a square with rounded corners, and a stainless-steel frame, extruded as if tracing the cut edge, had a cross-section like the wing of an airplane. It was not a sash, but a frame added to the outside.
After our gaze paused for a moment on that window frame, it quickly moved again to the tiles beside it, or to the darkness of a window set back in the wall. The experience of walking through the city is composed of a sequence of such small appearances. The single image that should have unified the whole becomes increasingly fragmented over time, eventually refracting to the point where it can no longer be fully grasped.
If these countless, elusive fragments were to be connected once again, what could offer a way to understand the image of the city from a particular point of view? Our answer was to hold an architectural exhibition in this neighborhood, and to let Ano bldg emerge within the memories of those who experienced the city.

To continue asking what architecture is also means to ask again what the self is. How do we perceive the world, and how do we express it? I believe that the purpose of doing so lies in sharing a way of seeing the world with unfamiliar others. The process of reconsidering oneself through an understanding of others overlaps with architectural research and study itself.

Ano bldg was an architectural exhibition created to produce such an experience.
This book is a re-edited archive of the exhibition Ano bldg, held from July 22 to October 4, 2025, including its atmosphere and exhibited contents. It is not a record intended to explain a completed architectural proposal, but a record of the process through which our way of seeing architecture itself changed as we walked through Yokoyama-cho, took photographs, made models, and rearranged the exhibition.
