Season 1: In Search of Abstraction / 
Exhibition Caption:


F. Collage Drawing

Among the fragments obtained through the research, there were elements that drew our attention before they could be organized as form or style, yet that certainly seemed to help architecture come into being. At times, they even appeared excessive or meaningless. By inheriting these fragments with their otherness intact, and reconstructing them as a single elevation, we attempted to discover a new order within the city.

In order to abstract the six surrounding buildings, we searched for new forms while retaining as much of their concrete information as possible. By giving meaning to the fragments obtained from the surrounding buildings, and by layering architectural history and the history of the city onto them, the maker’s interpretation shifts into new relationships between things.

A thick, large silver sash emphasizes itself, making a single individual window stand out. By layering continuous horizontal sliding doors onto it, the meaning of the window is both diluted and strengthened, and a façade appears that does not limit the functions inside.
Such layers of things produce multiple interpretations within a single form, allowing us to understand relationships between things, and between things and people, in a more complex way than the one-to-one causal relationships through which we had previously perceived them.

Our gaze seems to cut out fragments of things, and to compose space by connecting and relating them to one another. As the fragments of the building in front, the fragments of adjacent buildings, and the fragments of Ano bldg relate to and continue into one another, an abstraction of the city emerges within memory. It is built upon the layering of images with complex relationships, and may indirectly influence the way this city is recognized.
