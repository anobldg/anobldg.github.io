Season 2: From Fragments to the Whole / 
Exhibition Caption:


Observing Interstices

The interstice between the wall and the door belongs to the wall. Nevertheless, it takes on the color of the door, and changes its appearance as the door opens and closes.
When the door is closed: the interstice appears to protrude from the wall, separated as a single fragment. (Fragmentation / Separation)
When the door is open: the interstice is absorbed as part of the door, and integrated into the whole. (Wholeness / Integration)

The interstice created between the column and the sash is objectified, as if framing the sash. Its form behaves like part of the column, and seems almost too autonomous for something that is, in fact, an interstice. This area, emerging between the sash and the column, neither separates the two nor simply connects them, but contains the possibility of a new relationship.

We want to search for interstices that retain a certain degree of abstraction, appearing in the in-between condition between separation and integration.
