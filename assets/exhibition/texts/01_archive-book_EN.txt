Ano Bldg Archive / Overview:


“Ano Bldg” is an architectural exhibition project set in Nihonbashi Yokoyama-cho. Through the observation of fragments found in the town, the project reconstructs them into a single architectural proposal: Ano Bldg.

This website, “Ano Bldg Archive,” is an archival website documenting the exhibition “Ano Bldg.” Through exhibition photographs, drawings, models, sketches, and texts, it records the contents of the exhibition as it developed through its three phases: the first phase, “In Search of Abstraction”; the second phase, “Perceiving the Whole from Fragments”; and the third phase, “Reading Architecture Exhibition.”

Rather than substantially rewriting or refining the exhibits after the fact, this website is organized so that the structure of the exhibition itself can be traced through the materials presented in the venue.

The archive book, Ano Bldg, is a separate publication produced after the exhibition. It brings together newly created texts, drawings, sketches, and photographs, and reconfigures them as both a book to be read and an exhibition catalogue. It presents the research on Yokoyama-cho, the design process, and the thoughts developed through the exhibition in a format distinct from this website.
