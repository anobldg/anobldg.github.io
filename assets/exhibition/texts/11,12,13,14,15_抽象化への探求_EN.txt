Season 1: In Search of Abstraction / 
Exhibition Caption:


E. Organizing the Given Conditions

The site is located behind this exhibition venue. In this city, where large-scale redevelopment and long-standing small and medium-sized wholesalers coexist, we were searching for a way to affirm the present without placing one above the other. The result of that search was the research described in C.
When thinking about architecture, we need to face the conditions given by the site and its urban environment. Rather than forcing theory, research, and form into a single consistent narrative, we believe that an honest attitude toward architecture lies in accepting their discontinuities and overlaps, while still imagining the building as a whole.
The points to which we paid particular attention are organized below.

・Lot Consolidation and the Pressure of Redevelopment
The hypothetical site measures 460.58㎡ and faces only the road on the east side. Many surrounding sites have similar shapes, and they tend to be consolidated with sites on the opposite side, transforming through redevelopment into large-scale buildings with access from two directions. The proposal for Ano bldg is also an attempt to resist this tendency, and to consider a building that can stand as an independent plot, not absorbed into the form of the block, by forming strong relationships with the six surrounding buildings.

・The Difficulty of Changing the Use of Wholesale Buildings
The traditional wholesale buildings often found in Yokoyama-cho are characterized by access from only one street, narrow frontages, and deep plans. Because they have difficulty satisfying the legal requirements for natural lighting, it is difficult to convert them into habitable rooms. Many of their stairs are also straight-run stairs designed for efficiency, and structural constraints and the securing of habitability are constantly in conflict. For this reason, many examples use the roadside portion as habitable rooms and the rear portion as storage.

・Equipment Renewal and Floor Height Issues
Due to road setback regulations, the upper floors require setbacks. In addition, contemporary air-conditioning and drainage equipment has become larger, and conventional floor heights place limits on renovation. Choosing new construction is also a rational decision in order to comprehensively satisfy structural, equipment, and legal conditions.

・Minimum Housing Area Standards and Rent
Rent levels in Tokyo have been rising, making it difficult for young people with little savings to live in the central city. At the same time, by referring to the national minimum housing area standards, such as 25㎡ for a single-person household and 50㎡ for a family of four, we thought it necessary to search for the smallest possible forms of housing that could realistically exist within the city.

Based on these constraints and conditions, Ano bldg was planned as an eight-story mixed-use building. Floors 1 and 2, and floors 6 to 8, have a floor height of 4,000mm, while floors 3 to 5 have a floor height of 3,200mm. Units are arranged around a central void. Each dwelling unit is based on a basic module of 25.7㎡, and through the grid and wet-area core, the plan can be distributed into various unit types of 25.7㎡, 35.3㎡, 40.7㎡, and 51.3㎡.
To satisfy natural lighting requirements, a window-front open space of 3,200mm is secured in the depth direction of the site for habitable rooms on the third floor and above. This is effective as a natural lighting plan that does not depend on the distance from neighboring sites, and it is a flexible proposal that satisfies the conditions of the Building Standards Act while anticipating conversion to various future uses.
A straight-run staircase was also placed in the central void. The total area of habitable rooms on the third floor is 198.81㎡, clearing the legal condition that permits a straight-run staircase as long as the floor directly above does not exceed 200㎡. This is the most rational choice for simultaneously establishing the grid, the core arrangement, and the dwelling unit areas, while also being a form connected to the memory of this city.

From these functional and problem-solving perspectives, a single wireframe
emerged. This becomes a set of auxiliary lines for the fragments that will be layered from here, and appears as the compositional order of Ano bldg.
