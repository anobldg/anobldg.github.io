G. How the Space of Ano bldg Is Used

The interior created in this way can respond flexibly to the lives of its users, the number of people in a household, and economic conditions by changing how the cores and grids are grouped. It can also adapt its use in response to future transformations of the city.

The three composite façades, which we understand as abstract, carry a layering of images that feels somehow nostalgic, yet certainly does not exist in the surrounding area. Although their relationship to function and ways of living is faint, various images overlap there, giving multiple meanings to the existence of things.

For example, if bread is displayed in front of the curved glass sash, it becomes a shop showcase; if favorite vintage clothes are arranged there, it becomes a bay window for a dwelling that expresses individuality.

The space projecting into the window-front open space, shared between two dwelling units in order to bring in light, has a personal scale. Yet, as it appears to continue in the depth direction, it also allows one to sense the presence of others.

The large window that brings in light also takes in the light and shadow created by the projecting wall. It protrudes beyond the core, creating a state that cannot be explained by brightness alone.

By practicing daily life around a minimal core, partitions are minimized, making it possible to create a space with ambiguous distinctions within an urban environment where public and private are clearly divided.
