Exhibition as a Medium for Architecture

The exhibition Ano Bldg enters its third period, following the first and second exhibition periods.
In this exhibition, a new 1/20 scale section model, four chairs, and four booklets are placed within the venue. In the previous exhibitions, the emphasis was placed on presenting the architectural proposal itself. In the third period, however, we expand our perspective to the relationships these exhibited objects create within the space, and attempt to form a “reading architecture exhibition” in which the model, chairs, and booklets work together as one.

Through this composition, we aim to create a space for thinking more deeply about the city and architecture.
Ano Bldg is a fictional mixed-use building proposed for a hypothetical site along Yokoyama-cho Odori, located behind this exhibition venue. Its design refers to compositional methods found in abstract painting. In the process of developing two-dimensional operations of images into the three-dimensional space of architecture, the central theme became a reconsideration of how form is generated.
The four booklets produced alongside the exhibition contain four perspectives that underlie both the design and the exhibition.

1. From Fragments to the Whole
2. An Exploration of Abstraction
3. Ano BLDG. and Yokoyamacho
4. Reading Architecture Exhibition

Each booklet has its own independent theme, yet as they are read, they connect with one another, and a path of thought appears between seeing and reading. Through this process, subtle changes may also occur in the way the model and the scenery of the city are perceived.
We hope visitors will pick up the booklets, sit down on the chairs, and look at the model as if looking at a painting.
Within the intersection of reading and seeing, we hope that a landscape for thinking about the city and architecture will emerge.
