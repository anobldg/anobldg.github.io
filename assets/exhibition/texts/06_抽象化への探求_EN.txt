Season 1: In Search of Abstraction / 
Exhibition Caption:


A. Ano bldg

When we give form to architecture, or when we perceive something, we are always selecting a fragment of the world. We cannot deal with everything that appears before us; space is composed through what we choose, and through how we relate those things to one another.

We chose the word “abstraction” to describe this process. As long as we try to make something in this world, abstraction is unavoidable. And yet, abstraction is often treated as if it were a simplified sign. Is it not possible to abstract a subject without stripping it of its information?

In this exhibition, we look back self-critically at the way abstraction has been handled, and attempt to redefine it as a method for making architecture. We want to practice abstraction as a way to accept, and even enjoy, the fact that we can only ever understand things in fragments.
