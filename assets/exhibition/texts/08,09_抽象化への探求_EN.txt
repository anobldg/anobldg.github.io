Season 1: In Search of Abstraction / 
Exhibition Caption:


C. How the City Appears

This research for Ano bldg begins with the question of shared attributes in abstraction, as discussed in B. Inspired by Cézanne’s way of painting apples and oranges, it takes as its clue an attitude of repeated observation, experimentation, and trial and error.

The research focuses on six buildings in total: three buildings directly facing the site, and three adjacent buildings. Through elevation collages and models, we attempted to examine our own way of seeing the world. Using the iPhone 11 we normally use, we moved very close to the buildings and collected their fragments. We then connected those fragments again, attempting to perceive each building as a whole. This was a method for reconstructing the streetscapes and architecture that appear to us in everyday life.

When we try to fit an entire building into a single photograph, the building captured there feels somehow static, as if it has lost its life. By being contained within the photograph, the sensations we had previously grasped intuitively seemed to disappear, and the building seemed to turn into an object of observation. What we are trying to see is not the building as an object. It is the image of a building with atmosphere: one whose expression changes according to time, the condition of the sky, the way light falls on it, the angle from which it is seen, and the distance from which it is viewed.

The elevation collage became a means of recognizing the difference between the parts of existing buildings that are actually visible and the parts we understand conceptually. The model, meanwhile, became a device for spatially organizing that way of seeing. These methods are close to the way we perceive everyday scenery, and became clues for understanding the appearance of architecture once again as atmosphere.
